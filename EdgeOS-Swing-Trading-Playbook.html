Position Size Formula (ATR-Validated)
Size = (Account Risk %) ÷ (Stop Distance in ATR units × Price)
Source: Wilder, J.W. (1978), New Concepts in Technical Trading Systems. ATR-based stops adapt to actual volatility rather than arbitrary percentage thresholds.

Example — 1% risk rule ($100,000 account, 1 ATR stop):
Stock at $50 · ATR = $1.50 · Stop at $48.50 (1 ATR below entry)
Risk = $1.50 · Position size = $1,000 ÷ $1.50 = 666 shares = $33,300 notional

Three-line conviction (1.75× standard):
Position size = 666 × 1.75 = 1,165 shares · Risk still capped at 1% account
The 1% Rule — Capital Preservation Doctrine

Professional swing traders consistently apply 1–2% maximum account risk per trade. A 10-trade losing streak at 1% risk = 9.56% drawdown — recoverable. At 10% risk per trade: 3 losses = 27% drawdown, requiring a 37% gain just to break even.

Multi-line conviction increases position size — it does not increase risk %. The stop is always placed at the technical invalidation level (SATY trigger, prior support, dip buy milestone). Size is determined by how many shares fit within the 1% risk envelope given that stop.

R4 A New Interpretation of Information Rate

Authors: John L. Kelly Jr.

Source: Bell System Technical Journal, Vol. 35, No. 4, 1956, pp. 917–926. Extended by MacLean, Thorp & Ziemba (2011), The Kelly Capital Growth Investment Criterion, World Scientific.

Recognition: Applied by Warren Buffett, Bill Gross, and institutional quant funds globally · Confirmed optimal by Monte Carlo simulation (Frontiers in Applied Mathematics and Statistics, 2020)

FINDING
The Kelly Criterion determines the fraction of capital to risk per trade to maximise long-term geometric wealth growth: f* = (p/l)(1 − (1−p)/p × l/g). Full Kelly maximises growth but creates high drawdown. Fractional Kelly (half-Kelly at 50% of optimal) reduces volatility more than proportionally relative to expected growth, offering the optimal practical trade-off. Over-betting (risk more than Kelly) reliably leads to ruin over a large sample.
Playbook Application: Multi-line conviction sizing is Kelly mathematics made operational. When three independent production lines agree on the same stock, the probability p of the outcome increases — raising the Kelly-optimal fraction. The 1% risk rule (applied to all tiers) represents a conservative fractional Kelly approach appropriate before personal win/loss ratios are established through journaling.
R5 New Concepts in Technical Trading Systems — ATR Methodology

Authors: J. Welles Wilder Jr.

Source: Trend Research, 1978. ATR-based position sizing now standard in professional swing trading.

Recognition: Average True Range remains the universal practitioner standard for volatility-adjusted stop placement

FINDING
Stop-loss levels placed at technical invalidation points (support, resistance, moving average levels) produce superior outcomes versus arbitrary percentage stops. ATR-based stops adapt to actual volatility: high-volatility stocks receive wider stops in ATR units, low-volatility stocks receive tighter stops — maintaining consistent risk across all positions.
Playbook Application: EdgeOS SATY (Swing ATR Trigger) system directly implements ATR-based trigger levels. The dip buy milestones (9/13-below system) and SATY trigger prices replace arbitrary stop placement with mathematically derived levels. Position size formula: Size = (Account × Risk%) ÷ (Stop Distance). This formula appears in every tier's playbook.
